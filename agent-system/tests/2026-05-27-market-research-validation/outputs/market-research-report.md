## Audience Pain Points

1. Phone video looks shaky or unprofessional.
2. Beginner creator records alone and needs stable framing.
3. Small-space home setup needs cheap, compact gear.
4. New seller/creator does not know which gear matters first.
5. User fears wasting money on unnecessary accessories.
6. User needs practical checklist before buying: height, clamp size, angle, stability, desk/floor use.

Evidence quality: inferred from provided scenario, sample angles, and previous pillars. Confidence: medium-low.

## Competitor & Content Patterns

Observed/hypothesized patterns from provided context:

1. Problem-solution content likely stronger than deal-first content.
   - Signal: `shaky-video-problem` and `beginner-guide` outperformed `deal-alert` in sample.
2. Beginner education angle may fit both TikTok and Facebook.
   - Signal: F1 beginner-guide had highest CVR in sample.
3. Deal-alert angle is risky without current price/voucher proof.
   - Signal: F2 had 0 orders and deal claims need live offer proof.
4. Practical setup/checklist content is safer than “best tripod” claims.
   - Reason: avoids unsupported superiority claims.
5. Before/after framing demonstration is promising but needs actual creative asset or approved product use.
   - Risk: cannot imply personal use without confirmation.

Evidence quality: small sample + inference. Confidence: low-to-medium.
